progressiveIndexOf

Type:{.prefix}

Structural{.info}

Categories:{.prefix}

Selection{.info}

find, locate, where{.purpose}

        R=X progressiveIndexOf Y

R is Integer being the positions in which the items of Y are found in X. Once an item in Y has been found in X, it is no longer available for subsequnet items in Y to match. Thus items in X are "used up" as they are found. This is in distinction to →[indexOf] which will match all equal items in Y to the first occurance in X.

See also: →[indexOf] →[lookup]

Examples:

              'A,A,B,C,D'  progressiveIndexOf 'A,A,A,B,C,D,E'

↓0   │
│1   │
│5   │
│2   │
│3   │
│4   │
│5   │
