Critical Values

Instead of looking up values in a table, you can use the critical value operator to obtain critical values from the Normal, Student-t, ChiSquare and F Distributions.

Normal Distribution

Find the critical value which is greater than 95% of all standard normal values.

              normal criticalValue > .95
┌───────────────┐
│1.6448534002834│
└Float──────────┘

Right tailed critical value of the Standard Normal Distribution:

              normal criticalValue < .10
┌───────────────┐
│1.2815518369953│
└Float──────────┘

Two-tailed critical value of the Standard Normal Distribution:

              normal criticalValue ne .05
┌───────────────┐
│1.9599627284195│
└Float──────────┘

A mandatory competency test for high school sophomores has a normal distribution with a mean of 400 and a standard deviation of 100. The top 3% of students receive $500. What is the minimum score you would need to receive the award?

              400 100 normal criticalValue < .03
┌───────────────┐
│588.07925874944│
└Float──────────┘

The bottom 1.5% of students must go to summer school. What is the minimum score you would need to stay out of this group?

              400 100 normal criticalValue > .015
┌───────────────┐
│182.99110888231│
└Float──────────┘
